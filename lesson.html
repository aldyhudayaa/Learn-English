Grammar Rule

The present simple tense is used for regular actions.

  • I go to school every day.
  • She plays the guitar.

Past Simple Tense

Used to describe actions that happened in the past.

  • He walked to the store yesterday.
  • They watched a movie last night.

Future Tense

Used to talk about future events.

  • I will visit my grandmother tomorrow.
  • She is going to bake a cake.

Present Continuous Tense

Used for actions happening right now.

  • We are studying English.
  • He is playing football.

Modal Verbs

Used to express ability, possibility, permission, or advice.

  • She can swim very well.
  • You should eat more vegetables.

Passive Voice

Used when the doer of the action is unknown or unimportant.

  • The cake was eaten by the children.
  • The window was broken.
